## TOPIC 1: BUSINESS PLAN

### Part 1: Introduction
**Introduce the group's business idea:**
* Product (service) name.
* Key characteristics.
* Target customer demographic and target market.

### Part 2: Business Plan Details
**Present specific steps of the plan (based on the planning procedure in the Management textbook):**
* **Environmental Analysis:** Analyze the internal environment (conditions of the business entity) and the external environment.
    * *Models to use:* SWOT model or M. Porter's 5 Forces model.
* **Forecasting:** Forecast future trends.
* **Risk Anticipation:** Anticipate potential risks.
* **Feasibility:** Utilize experiences or compiled data to demonstrate the feasibility of the plan.

> **Important Note:** Identify a suitable market, aligning the scale of the plan accordingly. It is recommended to keep the scale small to fit the group's circumstances (considering yourselves as a small business entity).

### Part 3: Supporting Plans
* **Highlight limitations:** Discuss limitations of the business plan (optional, not mandatory).
* **Anticipate potential risks (supporting plan):**
    * The supporting plan ensures the success of the main plan, not replacing it (If Part 2 follows the 8 steps of planning, this content is likely covered).
    * Forecast risks and unfavorable factors that may arise during plan implementation, along with coping strategies.
    * Multiple supporting plans may be considered.

## TOPIC 2: LEADERSHIP STYLE

**1. Introduction:**
* Define leadership and briefly introduce the classification of leadership styles.

**2. Leadership Styles:**
* Present various leadership styles and their characteristics.
* Classify your favorite manager's leadership style.

**3. Case Study:**
* Analyze the leadership style of your favorite manager.
* Include real-life examples or scenarios showcasing their leadership approach.

**4. Conclusion:**
* Draw lessons from the case study.
* Summarize key insights and takeaways regarding effective leadership.
